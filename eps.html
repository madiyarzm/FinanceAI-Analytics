Earnings Per Share (EPS)

A fundamental metric that measures a company's profitability on a per-share basis. It's a key indicator used by investors to assess a company's financial health and value.

Basic EPS Formula

EPS = Net Income - Preferred Dividends / Weighted Average Common Shares

Basic vs Diluted

Basic EPS uses outstanding shares, while diluted EPS includes potential shares from convertible securities and options.

Time Periods

TTM (Trailing Twelve Months) EPS uses the last 12 months of data. Forward EPS uses projected earnings.

Quality Factors

Consider earnings quality, one-time items, and accounting methods when evaluating EPS.

Interpreting EPS

Growing EPS

Indicates improving profitability and potential for stock price appreciation.

Declining EPS

May signal business challenges or increased shares diluting earnings.

Volatile EPS

Could indicate cyclical business or unstable earnings stream.
