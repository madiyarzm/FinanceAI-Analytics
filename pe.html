Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio

A key valuation metric that helps investors determine if a stock is overvalued or undervalued by comparing its price to its earnings.

The P/E Ratio Formula

P/E Ratio = Stock Price / Earnings Per Share

Example Calculation

Stock Price: $50

Earnings Per Share: $2.50

P/E Ratio = $50 / $2.50 = 20

This means investors are willing to pay $20 for every $1 of earnings.

What It Tells Us

A higher P/E suggests investors expect higher earnings growth.

A lower P/E might indicate a company is undervalued or has limited growth prospects.

Key Considerations

Industry Context

P/E ratios vary significantly by industry. Compare companies within the same sector.

Time Horizon

Consider both trailing (past) and forward (projected) P/E ratios for a complete picture.

Growth Stage

High-growth companies often have higher P/E ratios due to future earnings potential.
