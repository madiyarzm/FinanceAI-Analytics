Understanding Beta (β)

Beta measures a stock's volatility relative to the overall market. It indicates how sensitive a stock's returns are to market movements.

Beta Scale

Low Beta (β < 1)

If market goes up 10%:

Stock with β = 0.5 goes up 5%

Examples: Utilities, Consumer Staples

Market Beta (β = 1)

If market goes up 10%:

Stock with β = 1 goes up 10%

Examples: Large-cap index funds

High Beta (β > 1)

If market goes up 10%:

Stock with β = 1.5 goes up 15%

Examples: Tech stocks, Small-caps

Beta Calculation

β = Covariance(Stock Return, Market Return) / Variance(Market Return)

Typically calculated using 5 years of monthly returns against a market index like the S&P 500

Key Points to Remember

Market Movement

Beta shows how much a stock typically moves in relation to the broader market.

Risk Indicator

Higher beta means higher potential returns but also higher risk.

Historical Measure

Beta is calculated using historical data and may not predict future volatility.
