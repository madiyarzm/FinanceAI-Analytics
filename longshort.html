Long vs Short Positions

Understanding the two fundamental ways to trade stocks: going long (buying) and going short (selling borrowed shares).

Long Position

Definition: Buying a stock with the expectation it will increase in value

Maximum Loss: Limited to initial investment

Maximum Gain: Unlimited potential upside

When to Use: When you expect the stock price to rise

Short Position

Definition: Borrowing shares to sell, hoping to buy back at a lower price

Maximum Loss: Unlimited potential loss

Maximum Gain: Limited to stock falling to zero

When to Use: When you expect the stock price to fall

How Short Selling Works

1

Borrow Shares

Borrow shares from a broker (paying interest)

2

Sell Shares

Sell borrowed shares at current market price

3

Wait for Price Drop

Wait for stock price to fall (hopefully)

4

Buy Back & Return

Buy shares at lower price and return to broker

Key Considerations

Risk Levels

Short selling carries higher risk due to unlimited potential losses if the stock price rises significantly.

Margin Requirements

Short selling requires a margin account and may have higher maintenance requirements.

Timing

Short positions often require more precise timing due to borrowing costs and unlimited risk.
